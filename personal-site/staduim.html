Ivory Coast, also known as Côte d'Ivoire, is home to several impressive stadiums that play a crucial role in the nation's sporting culture and international events. These stadiums serve as hubs for various sports, including football (soccer), which is immensely popular in the country. Here are some notable stadiums in Ivory Coast:

Stadium of Bouaké: (Stade De La Paix)

Bouaké, along with Abidjan, served as a host city for the 1984 Africa Cup of Nations. It is located in the central part of the country, 350 km from the economic capital, and 100 km from Yamoussoukro, the political capital.

Stadium of Korhogo

Korhogo, known as the "Pôrô" city, named after a tradition of the local Sénoufo ethnic group, is the capital of the Savanes region. It is located approximately 207 km from Mali and 242 km from Burkina Faso, with an estimated population of around 300,000 inhabitants.

Stadium of Félix Houphouët-Boigny

The birthplace of the first President of the Republic, the late Félix Houphouët-Boigny, Yamoussoukro serves as the political and administrative capital of Côte d'Ivoire.

Stadium of Ebimper

Alassane Ouattara Stadium, commonly known as the Olympic Stadium of Ebimpé and formerly as the National Stadium of the Ivory Coast, is a multi-purpose stadium in Ebimpé and Anyama, in northern Abidjan. It opened in 2020..
