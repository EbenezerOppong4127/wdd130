Ivory Coast, or Côte d'Ivoire, is a country in West Africa known for its rich and diverse culinary traditions. The local dishes of Ivory Coast reflect the nation's cultural diversity and abundance of fresh ingredients. Here are some delicious local dishes you might encounter in Ivory Coast:

Local Dish Braised fish

Foutou: Foutou is a staple Ivorian dish made from yam, plantains, or cassava. It's pounded into a dough-like consistency and served with a variety of soups and stews, such as groundnut (peanut) sauce, palm nut soup, or okra soup. Kedjenou: Kedjenou is a slow-cooked stew that's made with chicken, and sometimes other meats like goat or guinea fowl. The meat is simmered with tomatoes, eggplant, and various spices, creating a rich, flavorful dish.

Local dish or cultural dish

Alloco: Alloco is another favorite Ivorian street food. It features fried ripe plantains, typically served with a spicy tomato and chili pepper sauce. Alloco is a sweet and spicy delight that's a must-try for visitors. Klouikloui: Klouikloui is a traditional Ivorian dish made from fermented millet. It's often served with peanut or palm nut sauce, offering a unique and hearty taste. Grilled Goat Meat: Grilled goat meat is a common delicacy in Ivory Coast. The meat is marinated with spices and herbs, then grilled to perfection. It's a popular dish enjoyed at social gatherings and celebrations. Aloko: Aloko is a dish made from fried plantains, similar to Alloco, but with a different preparation. It is served with a tomato and chili pepper sauce or a spicy groundnut sauce.

Attiéké is a popular side dish in Côte d'Ivoire made with grated cassava and is very similar in taste and consistency to couscous. A common street-vended food is alloco, which is ripe plantain banana fried in palm oil, spiced with a spicy sauce made of onions and chili.
